## Section 2: Polite Greetings in Daily Life (3 minutes)

**Objective:** Learn polite and commonly used greetings.

In Mandarin, greetings often change depending on the time of day. While **nǐ hǎo (你好)** works in most situations, there are more natural phrases used in everyday conversation.

Another polite greeting is **nín hǎo (您好)**.  
- **nín** is a respectful form of *you*, often used with elders or in formal situations.

**Check Question:**  
When would you use **nín hǎo** instead of **nǐ hǎo**?

---

## Section 3: Saying Good Morning (3 minutes)

**Objective:** Learn how to say “good morning” in Mandarin.

To say good morning, you say **zǎo (早)** or **zǎo ān (早安)**.  
- **zǎo** means *early* and is very common in casual settings  
- **zǎo ān** is more formal and polite

In everyday life, many people simply say **zǎo** in the morning.

**Check Question:**  
Which greeting is more casual: **zǎo** or **zǎo ān**?

---

## Section 4: Saying Good Night (3 minutes)

**Objective:** Learn how to say “good night” in Mandarin.

To say good night, you say **wǎn ān (晚安)**.  
- **wǎn** means *evening*  
- **ān** means *peace* or *calm*

This phrase is usually said when going to sleep, not as a general evening greeting.

**Check Question:**  
When do you say **wǎn ān** in Mandarin?

---

## Section 5: Numbers 6–10 (3 minutes)

**Objective:** Recognize Mandarin numbers from six to ten.

Here are numbers six to ten in Mandarin:

- Six: **liù (六)**  
- Seven: **qī (七)**  
- Eight: **bā (八)**  
- Nine: **jiǔ (九)**  
- Ten: **shí (十)**  

Mandarin numbers are logical and build on each other, which makes counting easier as you learn more.

**Check Question:**  
How do you say the number eight in Mandarin?
