# Beginner Chinese Lesson 2 (15 Minutes)

## Section 1: Time-Based Greetings (2 minutes)

**Objective:** Learn how greetings change based on the time of day.

**Topic**  
In Mandarin, greetings often change depending on the time of day, similar to English. Using the correct greeting shows politeness and awareness of context. These expressions are common in daily conversations and are easy to recognize.

**Check Question:**  
Why is it useful to learn time-based greetings in Mandarin?

---

## Section 2: Saying Good Morning (4 minutes)

**Objective:** Learn how to say good morning in Mandarin.

**Topic**  
To say good morning in Mandarin, you say **zǎo ān (早安)**.  
- **zǎo** means *early*  
- **ān** means *peace* or *well*

A shorter and very common version is simply **zǎo (早)**, especially in casual or friendly situations, such as at school or work.

**Check Question:**  
What is the short, casual way to say good morning in Mandarin?

---

## Section 3: Saying Good Afternoon and Good Evening (3 minutes)

**Objective:** Learn polite greetings for later in the day.

**Topic**  
Good afternoon in Mandarin is **xià wǔ hǎo (下午好)**, and good evening is **wǎn shàng hǎo (晚上好)**. These greetings are polite and are often used in more formal situations or when speaking to someone you do not know well.

**Check Question:**  
Which greeting would you use in the evening?

---

## Section 4: Saying Good Night (3 minutes)

**Objective:** Learn how to say good night in Mandarin.

**Topic**  
To say good night in Mandarin, you say **wǎn ān (晚安)**. This phrase is usually used when someone is going to sleep, not as a general evening greeting. It expresses care and politeness.

**Check Question:**  
When do you use *wǎn ān* in Mandarin?

---

## Section 5: Saying Please (3 minutes)

**Objective:** Learn how to say please in Mandarin.

**Topic**  
The most common way to say please in Mandarin is **qǐng (请)**. It is used before a request to make it more polite. For example, **qǐng zuò (请坐)** means *please sit*. Politeness words are important in respectful communication.

**Check Question:**  
What word do you use in Mandarin to say please?

---

## Section 6: Review and Reflection (2 minutes)

**Objective:** Review what you learned in this lesson.

**Topic**  
In this lesson, you learned how to greet people in the morning, afternoon, evening, and at night, as well as how to say please in Mandarin. These expressions help you sound polite and natural in everyday interactions.

**Final Question:**
